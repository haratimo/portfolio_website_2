Best–Worst Scaling for product decisions

28 August 2025 · 6 min read

Best–Worst Scaling (BWS) helps teams prioritise features by asking participants to pick the most and least valuable item from a small set. It reduces noise and fatigue compared with long rating scales.

When to use it

  • Comparing feature bundles or pricing attributes
  • Ranking error messages or copy for clarity
  • Assessing perceived value of research outputs

How to run quickly

  1. Define 12–24 items. Remove duplicates and extremely similar items.
  2. Use 4-item choice sets with balanced exposure across participants.
  3. Target 8–12 sets per participant for a short, low-fatigue session.

What to report

  • Scores per item and relative ranks
  • Confidence intervals or bootstrapped uncertainty
  • Clear recommendations with trade-offs
